Critical Tensions & Null Results

Consistent with UBP research methodology, all tensions, anomalies, and potential falsifications are documented explicitly. A theory that only counts confirming evidence is not science.

Tension T-01 — No CME for Waden Hill Primary Driver
Issue: The M1.6 flare on April 28 (the designated primary print instruction) had no confirmed CME. The R1 blackout was via prompt electromagnetic radiation (X-ray/EUV) arriving at light speed — not via particle flux. The proposed ionospheric coupling mechanism (solar wind → magnetosphere → formation) requires a particle/field disturbance, not just prompt radiation.

Response: The M6.0 on April 26 also had no confirmed CME. However, a coronal hole high-speed stream (+CH HSS) was forecast to arrive late April 29 — this is a particle-carrying structure. The formation may have been "seeded" by the M6.0 EUV radiation setting up the ionospheric path, then "triggered" by the early edge of the +CH HSS arriving at the magnetosphere. This requires timing data from DSCOVR/ACE L1 solar wind monitors to verify. Unresolved
Tension T-02 — Bondip Hill During Solar Quiet Period
Issue: The first 2026 formation appeared during what appears to be a low-activity solar period, contradicting the direct correlation hypothesis.

Response: Three accommodations offered in §5. Most likely resolution is human agency for Bondip Hill. Must be treated as a null result until solar data for Apr 1–4 is acquired from definitive sources. Unresolved
Tension T-03 — The Base Rate Problem (No Control Data)
Issue: We know formations appeared during M-class solar windows. We do not know how often M-class windows occurred with no formation. If the Sun produces M-class events weekly (which it does during SC25 peak) and formations appear only 2–3 times per month during growing season, the coincidence rate is indistinguishable from chance without controlling for base rate.

Response: A full 2026 season correlation study requires: (a) logging every M+ solar event during the UK growing season (April–September), (b) recording formation or non-formation outcome for each, (c) applying a Fisher's exact test or similar. This is the key methodological requirement to move from "interesting correlation" to "statistically significant." Critical Gap
Tension T-04 — Human Crop Circle Makers Are Documented
Issue: Human teams have made crop circles on record since the 1970s (Doug Bower and Dave Chorley, confirmed), and professional circlemakers continue to operate. Many formations are definitively human-made.

Response: The UBP hypothesis does not deny human agency for the majority of formations. It claims that a subset — distinguished by specific Golay-compatible geometry, site selection, and solar event timing — is not human-made. This is a hard prediction: if all formations are human-made, no Golay-compatible geometry should preferentially occur during M+ solar windows at high-conductivity geological sites, any more than at other times. The base rate study in T-03 directly tests this. Testable
Tension T-05 — The Golay Mapping Is Conceptual, Not Derived
Issue: The mapping of formation geometry to Golay codeword weight (e.g., "dumbbell = weight-8 octad") is an analogical mapping, not a derived result of the Golay code mathematics. There is no proof that a D₂ geometry uniquely corresponds to a weight-8 codeword.

Response: This is the most fundamental scientific limitation of the current approach. The Golay correspondence must be formalised as a bijective mapping before claims about TGIC "forcing" specific geometries can be made. A candidate formalisation: each topologically distinct element of a formation (node, ring, shaft, spiral arm) maps to a bit position, with element presence = 1 and absence = 0. The resulting 24-bit vector can then be Hamming-distance-compared to the 4096 Golay codewords. If the distance is consistently zero or near-zero for formations identified as potentially solar-origin, this is a meaningful result. Framework Refinement Required
Tension T-06 — Kp 4.0 Claim Unverified for April 28
Issue: The existing study states "a sustained Kp 4.00 was recorded during the 21:00–00:00 UTC synoptic period on April 28." The Watchers forecaster noted conditions were expected to be "mostly quiet" returning by April 28, and the coronal hole HSS was not expected until late April 29.
